Moves: A Personal Fitness App

My Role
 Research, Interviews, Wireframes, High Fidelity Prototype, Usability Testing
Deliverables
 Wireframes, High Fidelity Prototype, Process Deck
Timeline
 January 2019 - March 2019
ABOUT MOVES

Moves is a mobile app designed to help the people who are going to the gym for the first time in their workout. The app is designed with basic and high contrast colors considering the color blind population.

GOALS
  • Help in building up confidence for the beginners
  • Give feedback to the beginners on correct body form and postures
  • Motivate active users for the workout

DESIGN CHALLENGE

How to make a mobile app visually appealing without relying on colors and images?

RESEARCH

The initial research was divided into three parts, Observations, Content Analysis and Interviews.

Observations and Content Analysis

I went to the Gym for 3 days to observe the general characteristics of people working out.
Some of the things that I focused on are:

  • Are the people working out alone or in groups?
  • Are they using any gadgets?
  • How often people are asking questions at the information desk?

I analyzed the content from AthleanX website and YouTube Channel and found out that the they collect data from the users by asking 11 questions and provides them a paid workout plan accordingly. Random workout sessions are free on YouTube.

Interview Insights

I took 7 interviews, 3 at the Gym and 4 at other locations. The key findings from the interview are as follows:

  • "It’s good if you have partner because they can motivate you and help you reach the next stage"
  • "When you are new, you need to know how your form is, how do you exactly work on the machine. Once you know how to do it, it doesn’t matter if the trainer is there"
  • "Watched Youtube videos but I haven’t used any application"
  • "I have to wait sometimes because it is crowded"

WIREFRAMES

Low Fidelity

I first made a low fidelity wireframe using pen-paper to get an idea of the content that I want to include in my design.

Medium Fidelity

The design started to come alive with the medium fidelity design. The major issues I was facing here were how to improve information hierarchy and the user flow.

1. After Onboarding Process

2. Workout Options

3. Weekly Sessions

4. Daily Sessions

  1. After Onboarding Process: After a 6 step login/sign up and Onboarding process this screen will appear. The content is supposed to calm down a beginner and add a little bit of humor.
  2. Workout Options: Selected icon is highlighted using a circle instead of just changing the color considering the color blind population.
  3. Weekly Sessions: The workout options are suggested based on the answers obtained in the onboarding process. Every workout is split into weekly sessions.
  4. Daily Sessions: If they have completed workout of a particular day, a signifier will tell them so. This will help them in building up confidence.

1. Motivation Screen

2. Today’s Workout

3. Equipment Page

4. AR / Buddy Mode

  1. Motivation Screen: This screen is designed to motivate users. It shows them the calories burned in a particular number of days The progress bar has a pattern instead of just different color.
  2. Today’s Workout: This screen shows the details about the workout to be done and the time required to complete it. After completing a particular workout, a “right mark” will appear as a signifier.
  3. Equipment Page: This page will specify the equipments required for a particular workout and will give another option to the user if they don’t have it.
  4. AR / Buddy Mode: The user can ask a friend to hold the camera pointing towards the user and if the body form is wrong, it will be indicated on the screen.

1. Listen and Watch

2. Podcasts

3. A Specific Podcast

  1. Listen and Watch: Music, Podcasts and Videos plays a very important role in motivating the users. A motivational talk from a particular person can prove very inspiring.
  2. Podcasts: Podcasts and music are very useful while working out as the user doesn’t need to pay a lot of attention.
  3. A Specific Podcast: The podcast playing currently is highlighted using an underline and the running track is also highlighted using color as well as a line below it.

High Fidelity Design

Work in progress

I finally tackled the information hierarchy problem visible in my Medium Fidelity Design. For my visual design, I used two different art directions: The Flash and Green Arrow. I made Mood boards, Inspiration boards and Style boards for both the directions and then decided to go with the "Flash" visual theme as it was based on heavy contrasts, motion and patterns.

REFLECTION

I realized that it is not very difficult to design for accessibility. Underlining the important points rather than just making it bold or changing colors, conveying the messages by using shapes and not just colors is the biggest takeaway. Also, as this was an individual project and I learned a lot of new techniques and how to utilize them considering time efficiency and deadlines.
